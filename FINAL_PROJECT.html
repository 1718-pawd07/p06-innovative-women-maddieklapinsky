Biography

Elena Lucrezia Cornaro Piscopia was the first woman to ever receive a PhD. She was born on June 5th, 1646 to a wealthy family in Venice, Italy. At a young age she began receiving tutoring by local priests. This was very uncommon at the time; many men were not even lucky enough to receive an education. She learned grammar, mathematics, science, music, philosophy, and theology. Piscopia also learned latin, Greek, Hebrew, Spanish, French, and Arabic. Elena Piscopia became very knowledgable and her father wanted her to be recognized for her intelligence. He insisted that she study at the University of Padua and receive a Doctorate of Theology. She was denied the opportunity to receive this but officials allowed her to apply for the Doctorate of Philosophy. On June 25, 1678 at the age of 32 she became the first woman to receive a PhD. The University of Padua did not award another woman with a PhD until 70 years later. Later, she decided that she did not want to get married and became a Benedictine oblate nun and spent the rest of her life helping the poor. Elena Lucrezia Cornaro Piscopia died at the age of 38 on July 26, 1648. She is buried in the Church of Santa Giustina in Padua, Italy. Today, Vassar College has a stained glass window in her honor, the University of Padua displays a statue, and many writers quote her in their works.
